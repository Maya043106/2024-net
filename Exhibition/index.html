Net Art Exhibition: Digital Nostalgia: Exploring where Technology intersects with Memory

This exhibition features three different net artworks that are centered around the theme of nostalgia. Focusing on the concept of nostalgia within the realm of digital art, different aspects of interpretations are represented for individuals throughout the exhibition. The first exhibition Camerons World is a collage that captures the aesthetic and cultural essence through reimagining the early 1990s and early 2000s. In the second exhibition Paper Rad uses drawings and multimedia works to create works of aesthetics of the past. Referencing visual cultures of the 1980s and 1990s, vibrant and colorful elements are used to create an environment that evokes the spirit of the era. Lastly, The Internet Arcade is a web tool that allows users to browse through 1,100 classic arcade games that can be played. Through the internet, these arcade games displayed online make it accessible for the audience to engage in. These different exhibitions represent a nostalgic blast from the past where digital multimedia can be a meaningful memory that was short lived but always remembered.
